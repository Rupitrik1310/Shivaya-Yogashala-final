# Sanskrit Shlokas in Shivaya Yogashala Website

## 5 Sacred Sanskrit Shlokas Integrated Throughout the Site

### 1. **Home Page - Hero Section**
**Shloka:** योगः चित्तवृत्ति निरोधः  
**Transliteration:** Yogaḥ Citta-Vṛtti-Nirodhaḥ  
**Meaning:** Yoga is the cessation of the fluctuations of the mind  
**Source:** Patanjali Yoga Sutras 1.2  
**Location:** HomePage.tsx - Hero section below CTA buttons

---

### 2. **Home Page - Sacred Elements Section**
**Shloka:** तद्योगानुशासनम्  
**Transliteration:** Tat Yogānuśāsanam  
**Meaning:** Now begins the teaching of Yoga  
**Source:** Patanjali Yoga Sutras 1.1  
**Location:** HomePage.tsx - Sacred practice section in highlighted box

---

### 3. **Home Page - CTA Section**
**Shloka:** योगः कर्मसु कौशलम्  
**Transliteration:** Yogaḥ Karmasu Kauśalam  
**Meaning:** Yoga is skill in action  
**Source:** Bhagavad Gita 2.50  
**Location:** HomePage.tsx - Call-to-action section in white backdrop box

---

### 4. **Footer - Sacred Mantras Section**
**Shlokas:**
1. **ॐ नमः शिवाय**  
   **Transliteration:** Om Namaḥ Śivāya  
   **Meaning:** I bow to Shiva, the inner Self  
   **Source:** Traditional Shaiva Mantra

2. **लोकाः समस्ताः सुखिनो भवन्तु**  
   **Transliteration:** Lokāḥ Samastāḥ Sukhino Bhavantu  
   **Meaning:** May all beings everywhere be happy and free  
   **Source:** Traditional Sanskrit Prayer

**Location:** Footer.tsx - Sacred mantras section above copyright

---

### 5. **About Page - Hero Section**
**Shloka:** स्थिरसुखमासनम्  
**Transliteration:** Sthira-Sukham-Āsanam  
**Meaning:** Asana should be steady and comfortable  
**Source:** Patanjali Yoga Sutras 2.46  
**Location:** AboutPage.tsx - Hero section below main description

---

## Design Integration

All Sanskrit shlokas are styled with:
- **Primary color** for the Sanskrit text (teal/blue)
- **Italic styling** to distinguish sacred text
- **Transliteration** in smaller text below
- **English meaning** for accessibility
- **Bordered boxes** or highlighted backgrounds for emphasis

## Spiritual Significance

These five shlokas represent:
1. **The Definition of Yoga** - What yoga truly is
2. **The Beginning** - Starting the sacred journey
3. **The Practice** - How to apply yoga in life
4. **The Devotion** - Connection to the divine (Shiva)
5. **The Foundation** - The essence of asana practice

Together, they create a complete spiritual framework for students beginning their yoga journey at Shivaya Yogashala.
